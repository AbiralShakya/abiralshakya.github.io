I am an undergraduate at Princeton studying electrical and computer engineering. I'm interested in data efficient world and robot behavior models, human and machine reasoning, and lightning fast hardware and systems.

I'm currently researching reasoning models advised by Professor Tri Dao. My research interests focus on hardware efficient and long context modelling, and intelligent spatial understanding. I also build in this space.

My friends and I started Tiger Tapeout to bring hardware culture to Princeton, we work on heterogenous compute and design custom silicon, kernels, and compilers.

Previously, I worked on RL and reasoning for verilog design integration at Architect Labs, firmware and drivers for ferroelectric memory at Kepler Computing, researched bulk spin polarization for dense computational logic and orbital angular momentum of optomechanical gyroscopes at Cornell, and programmed award winning autonomous robots.

P.S. I use a lot of sticky notes! I also make beats, debate ethics, listen to indie rap & french pop, and enjoy Pacific Northwest nature.
